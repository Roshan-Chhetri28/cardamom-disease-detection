Abstractt about the research: Large Cardamom (Amomum subulatum) is a high-value, shade-
loving spice crop predominantly cultivated in the hilly regions of India. The
crop is highly susceptible to diseases caused by the Cardamom Bushy Dwarf
Virus (CBDV) and the Large Cardamom Chirkey Virus (LCCV), which
manifest as Chirkey and Foorkey diseases. If not addressed promptly, these
diseases can severely damage or kill the plants, leading to significant economic
losses. Traditional methods of disease identification rely on manual inspections
and expert assessments, which are often time-consuming, labor-intensive, and
prone to inaccuracies. This paper presents a comparative study of Machine
Learning (ML) and Deep Learning (DL) algorithms for accurately classifying
healthy Large Cardamom plants and those infected with Chirkey and Foorkey
diseases. A primary dataset was collected directly from the plants&#39; natural
environment, ensuring the authenticity and variability of real-world conditions.
Advanced feature extraction techniques, including Gray-Level Co-occurrence
Matrix (GLCM), Local Binary Patterns (LBP), and Color Histograms, were
employed to capture key patterns and textures in the plant images. Furthermore,
data augmentation techniques were integrated to enhance the robustness of the
models by addressing the challenges posed by limited datasets. The study
evaluates various models using metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall,
confusion matrix, precision-recall (PR) curve, and Area Under the Receiver
Operating Characteristic (AUC-ROC) curve. Among the models tested,
Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) demonstrated superior performance,
achieving high classification accuracy and robust generalization. The analysis
underscores the potential of combining feature extraction, augmentation, and
CNN-based ensemble techniques for efficient and scalable Large Cardamom
disease identification.
